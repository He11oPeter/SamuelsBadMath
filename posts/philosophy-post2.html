The movie "Idiocracy," a comedy from 2006, presents a picture of a future in which humans have evolved to become comically stupid[cite: 2]. [cite_start]The premise looks something like this: society has gotten to the point where the educated are deciding to not have children and the stupid are reproducing more, thus through heredity each generation becomes on average less intelligent[cite: 3]. [cite_start]Now I must say that this is just a comedy, and although it does possess a basic understanding of heredity, it fails to acknowledge that general "smartness" is more determined in our current society by education and upbringing than it is by genetics[cite: 4].

[cite_start]

Without selective pressure, there will be no beneficial evolution[cite: 9]. [cite_start]I believe our current society lacks this pressure[cite: 14]. [cite_start]Almost everyone in developed countries is living until reproductive age, and individuals with higher education and income levels are having fewer children[cite: 15, 17]. [cite_start]Survival and reproduction are no longer difficult, and basic reproduction no longer seems capable of bringing us to the next stage of biological evolution[cite: 26]. [cite_start]The next stage is not about "perfecting" humans, but about tweaking human biology in specific ways in order to prepare people to handle challenges that previously were difficult or impossible to accomplish[cite: 27, 31].

1. [cite_start]Why we should care about the continued evolutionary progress of humans [cite: 40]

[cite_start]

The purpose of our actions is derived from the forces that create and govern us[cite: 42]. [cite_start]For those who understand evolution, these principles are the forces from which our objectives are derived[cite: 46]. [cite_start]Thus, our purpose is rooted in the necessity to pursue evolutionary success[cite: 47]. [cite_start]Failure to do so means a lineage ceases to exist[cite: 49]. [cite_start]Furthermore, we are programmed to feel positive emotions for what is evolutionarily good and negative emotions for what is bad[cite: 51]. [cite_start]While Aristotle identified happiness as a chief good, his deeper insight was that true human flourishing (eudaimonia) comes from fulfilling our unique function (ergon)[cite: 63]. [cite_start]Our most fundamental characteristic is the pursuit of knowledge for its own sake (sophia)[cite: 64, 65]. [cite_start]Evolutionary success and happiness are bootstrap mechanisms for a higher purpose: the attainment of Knowledge[cite: 65, 66].

Axiom 2: The purpose of a thing is determined by the things that create and govern it[cite: 75]. [cite_start]This is a simple recognition of cause and effect[cite: 76].

[cite_start]While evolution is a primary force that created the biological human, the capacity for abstract reason, scientific inquiry, and the accumulation of knowledge represents the most fundamental and unique characteristic of the human mind[cite: 93].

2. [cite_start]Why we should care about the continued progress via biological evolution [cite: 101]

[cite_start]

With the advent of modern gene editing technology, we now have the ability to create changes that can accelerate our ability to survive, grow, and create useful complexity[cite: 117]. [cite_start]The question is not whether we should make changes, but what changes we should make[cite: 118]. [cite_start]Framed by our ultimate purpose, the pursuit of gene editing becomes a tool for epistemic enhancement[cite: 119]. [cite_start]The goal is to refine and augment the biological "hardware" upon which all knowledge acquisition depends[cite: 120].

[cite_start]

Potential avenues for such enhancement include: [cite: 122]

    [cite_start]
  • Sensory Augmentation: Modifying our biology to perceive phenomena beyond our current sensory range[cite: 123].
    • [cite_start]
  • Cognitive Enhancement: Increasing the brain's neuroplasticity, memory consolidation, and pattern-recognition capabilities[cite: 124].
    • [cite_start]
  • Psychological Resilience: Altering our neurochemistry to be more resilient to cognitive biases, tribalism, and irrational fear[cite: 126].

3. [cite_start]Why human Biological evolution has stagnated [cite: 127]

[cite_start]

I believe that human biological evolution has stagnated[cite: 128]. [cite_start]For evolution to occur, you need diversity and selective pressure[cite: 130]. [cite_start]As human society has become more interconnected, we have prioritized the survival of all its members[cite: 131]. [cite_start]Combined with the fact that those considered successful (by metrics like higher education and income) are reproducing at a lower rate, it is hard to see how this pattern could lead to positive biological evolution[cite: 132]. [cite_start]This has created a "wisdom gap," where our technological power outstrips the cognitive and ethical capacity of our Stone Age minds to manage it wisely[cite: 141].

4. [cite_start]The Challenges of Pursuing Gene Editing as a Mechanism of Evolutionary Progress [cite: 143]

    [cite_start]
  • Alienation: Changing our biology could lead to a sense of alienation amongst members of society who have been modified in unique ways[cite: 147, 148].
    • [cite_start]
  • The Meaning of "Human": In the pursuit of evolutionary progression, we face issues that come from the fracturing of humans into many species[cite: 150, 151].
    • [cite_start]
  • Maintaining Diversity and Preventing Monoculture: If certain enhancements become highly desirable, social pressures could push toward a genetic monoculture, making our species more vulnerable[cite: 163, 164].
    • [cite_start]
  • Catastrophic Technical and Ethical Failures: An enhancement mistake is not just a health problem for one person; it could be a catastrophe for civilization, passed down through generations[cite: 166, 171].
    • [cite_start]
  • Discrimination and a Heritable Genetic Divide: A primary societal risk is that access to expensive enhancement technologies will be limited to the wealthy, creating a permanent, biological class system[cite: 173, 174].
    • [cite_start]
  • The Geopolitical Threat of a Genetic Arms Race: Gene-editing technology is a quintessential dual-use technology, classified as a potential Weapon of Mass Destruction (WMD)[cite: 177, 178].
    • [cite_start]
  • Mitigating Risk with Reversible Technologies: Emerging technologies like CRISPRoff, which add reversible chemical tags instead of altering DNA, offer a vital safety mechanism[cite: 184, 185, 186].

5. [cite_start]Suggested Guidelines For the Future of Gene Editing [cite: 188]

[cite_start]

While individual liberty is paramount, the scale of the challenges highlights the potential for rational individual choices to lead to collectively disastrous outcomes[cite: 190]. [cite_start]A robust framework must therefore create an ecosystem that supports responsible and equitable choices[cite: 191].

    [cite_start]
  • A Polycentric Governance Model: Rather than a single top-down authority, a resilient approach is a network of overlapping governance bodies, including national agencies, international conventions, and self-regulation by scientific organizations[cite: 193, 194].
    • [cite_start]
  • The Mandate for Public Deliberation: For a technology with such profound societal implications, choices should be informed by a broad societal conversation through structured processes like citizens' juries[cite: 197, 200].
    • [cite_start]
  • An International Framework for Security: The clear danger of a genetic arms race necessitates a specific international security regime to prevent the weaponization of the technology[cite: 201, 202].
    • [cite_start]
  • Policies to Ensure Equitable Access: To prevent a genetic class divide, proactive policies are needed, such as public investment in therapies, price regulations, and initiatives to diversify genomic databases[cite: 205, 207, 208].
    • [cite_start]
  • A Regulatory Preference for Reversibility: Governance can steer innovation toward safer pathways by creating a "fast track" for the development of reversible technologies like CRISPRoff, incentivizing industry to prioritize methods that mitigate profound ethical risks[cite: 209, 210, 211].
